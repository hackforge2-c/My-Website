02

Security Operations

SOC

Security Operations is the heart of an organisation's defence. These are the day-to-day activities performed by security teams to detect, respond to, and recover from threats.

📊
SIEM Monitoring
Collect and correlate logs from all systems. Detect anomalies in real-time. Tools: Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel, QRadar.
🕵️
Threat Hunting
Proactively search for threats that evade automated detection. Hypothesis-driven investigation using TTPs.
🚨
Incident Response
Detect → Contain → Eradicate → Recover → Learn. The structured process to handle security breaches.
🔍
Digital Forensics
Collect and preserve digital evidence. Analyse artefacts from disk, memory, and network captures.
⚠️
Vulnerability Assessment
Systematic scanning of systems to identify weaknesses before attackers do. Tools: Nessus, OpenVAS.
💀
Penetration Testing
Authorised simulated attacks to evaluate security posture. Black-box, white-box, and grey-box approaches.
🔴
Red Team
Offensive security team that simulates real-world attackers to test the organisation's defences.
🔵
Blue Team
Defensive security team. Monitors, detects, and responds to attacks. Works with SIEM, EDR, and IPS.
🖥️
EDR / XDR
Endpoint Detection and Response. Extended Detection and Response adds network, cloud, and email telemetry.
📡
NDR
Network Detection and Response. Analyses traffic patterns to detect lateral movement and C2 communication.

Common Cyber Attacks

Threats

Understanding how attackers operate is the first step in building effective defences. These are the most common attack types every security professional must know.

🎣
Phishing
Fraudulent emails/messages designed to steal credentials or deliver malware. #1 initial access vector.
Critical
🔐
Ransomware
Malware that encrypts victim files and demands payment for decryption. Can cripple entire organisations.
Critical
💉
SQL Injection
Inserting malicious SQL into input fields to manipulate databases. Leads to data theft and authentication bypass.
High
⚡
Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)
Injecting malicious JavaScript into web pages viewed by other users. Steals session cookies and credentials.
High
💥
DoS / DDoS
Overwhelming a server with traffic to make services unavailable. DDoS uses botnets of thousands of infected machines.
High
🔑
Brute Force
Systematically trying all possible passwords until one works. Countered by account lockouts and MFA.
Medium
⚠️ Remember: 91% of cyberattacks begin with a phishing email. User awareness training is your first line of defence.

Red Team vs Blue Team vs Purple Team

Operations

Cybersecurity teams are often organised into red, blue, and purple functions — each playing a different but essential role in protecting an organisation.

🔴
Red Team — Attackers
Simulates real-world adversaries. Performs penetration testing, phishing campaigns, physical intrusion tests. Goal: find what Blue Team misses.
🔵
Blue Team — Defenders
Monitors networks and systems, detects intrusions, responds to incidents. Operates SIEM, EDR, and firewall rules. Goal: detect and stop attackers.
🟣
Purple Team — Collaboration
Bridge between Red and Blue. Red shares attack techniques; Blue improves detection. Together they close security gaps faster.
🟡
Yellow Team — Builders
Developers and architects who build secure systems. Work with DevSecOps to embed security in the SDLC from the start.
